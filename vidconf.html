Connect through Video

What is it?

Video calling allows you to speak to your family and friends while also seeing their faces. The 2D version of your closest people is not as good as the 3D versions, but they are still pretty great!

Why might you need to know this?

So that you can learn how to join a video call if someone sends you an invite.

What will you need?

You only need your smartphone, tablet or laptop. Make sure your device is charged up as these calls can take up a lot of battery power!

What will you achieve?

As the rest of world battles to overcome the coronavirus pandemic, with social distancing becoming the new normal, people will increasingly find ways to stay connected and keep in touch with their love ones. With the advancement in technology, smartphones have a way of bringing us even closer to our family and friends by only a few touches of the screen in order to create that virtual audience.

How to do it

The following tips will go through how to log in to a Zoom meeting and get chatting with your nearest and dearest!

  1. Open Zoom on your phone and select the option to Join a Meeting
  2. Type in the meeting ID that was provided to you
  3. Click on the Join Meeting button
  4. Put your microphone and video on by clicking the two icons in the bottom left hand side corner
  5. Enjoy the call!

More video tools

The following are a handful of the web applications out there which gives us the video-conferencing experience provided we have them installed on our devices

  1. WhatsApp

    It's one of the most widely used video-conferencing tool right now. As long as it's been installed on both phones of the caller and receiver. WhatsApp have tremendously cut cost in long distance communication and it only gets better as time passes

    The back-to-back encryption security on the service is a caviar, as provides the necessary protection against third party intruders

  2. Imo

    With just a registered phone number and/email address, you can use Imo to make video-conferencing calls to just about anyone provided they have the web application installed on their smartphones or tablets

  3. Skype

    As the global pandemic have forced most companies, corporations to reconsider working within the confines of office spaces, Skype has been the go-to platform for co-workers to hold meetings/share presentations

    Skype is convenient for remote work, although it could be used by private individuals who have accounts set up on their service.
