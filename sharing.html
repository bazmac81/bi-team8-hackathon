How to safely share your family photos online

What is it?

This section of our tutorial helps you share your family photos with other family members safely over the internet.

Why might you need to know this?

Staying in touch with family members and keeping up to date with everyone through memories is a long-standing tradition. With our tutorial you will find out how to safely share your pictures with family members.

What will you need?

You will need your smartphone and the default photo app on the phone.

What will you achieve

You will be able to share photos with your contacts.

How to do it

Go to your phones photo app.

  1. Select a photo.
  2. Select the share button.
  3. Select a contact to share your photo with.
  4. Hit send.

Find out more about this topic

The big advantage of apps is they're simple to use—even for technophobes—and they let you grant access to family and friends. Many are free or charge a minimal fee. On the downside, technical support can be hard to come by and users run the risk of glitches or even shutdowns. For that reason, it's always a good idea to back up your photos in other secure places.

Apps that really offer privacy

There are dozens of photo-sharing apps out there, but not all thoroughly address privacy concerns. The picks below are both easy to use and truly off limits to others.

  1. 23 Snaps (Free): Founded when a father-in-waiting wanted a secure way to document and privately share the early moments of his young family, 23Snaps says its mission is to "create truly secure and private ways to share photos and videos with the people you love." The app, which you can access via phone, tablet, or Web browser, lets you organize your photos into collections or view them in a timeline, gallery or calendar view.
  2. Cluster (Free) First launched in 2013 and updated in 2015, the app is designed specifically for private group sharing—it doesn't even offer a public access option. It also has many of the same features as Facebook or Instagram, including likes and alerts. Better yet, you can create specific groups for photo sharing, whether it's one side of the family, a specific group of friends, or around a trip or an event.
